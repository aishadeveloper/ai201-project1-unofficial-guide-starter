Tip 1: Make Sure Your Program Mentor Is Reliable and Responsive

At Western Governor's University, your mentor is the person who has the ability to unlock additional courses once you complete the ones currently in your term. If you finish a class and want to immediately move into the next one, you are going to need a mentor who responds quickly to emails or messages. Ideally you want someone who gets back to you within a day. That way you're not sitting around waiting to start the next course while time in your term is passing. Most mentors are very responsive. Tip 2: Use Pre-Made Quizlet Decks

When you're studying for objective assessment courses, I cannot emphasize how incredibly time-saving this is. These are the courses that end up with a proctored exam. Students have already created study decks for these classes and using them can help you review key concepts way faster than starting from scratch. The first time I went through my program, I was trying to make the cards as I went and it just took up way too much time. Instead of rereading the entire textbook repeatedly, you can use those Quizlet decks to reinforce the terminology, definitions, and major concepts that you're going to see on those exams. It's not necessarily a replacement for understanding the material, but it is such a great effective way to review and save a lot of time.

Tip 3: For Performance Assessments, the Rubric Is Everything

The biggest mistake people make with performance assessment assignments is overthinking them. The graders are not evaluating how long your paper is or how fancy the writing sounds. What they really care about is whether you clearly addressed every single requirement in the rubric. If you structure your paper so it directly answers each rubric point, you will usually move through those assignments way faster.

One thing that can also help is running your paper through something like ChatGPT or another AI tool before submitting it, to make sure you have everything aligned from the rubric. It can do a kind of pre-grading. Grading can take a few days and revisions can add even more time, so it's helpful to catch things you might have missed before submitting. If a paper gets sent back, you fix the issues and resubmit, and then it takes another few days. That can add up to six days of waiting around. Use AI to your advantage — obviously don't use it to cheat, but use it to proofread and verify you have everything aligned correctly before you submit your work.

Tip 4: Alternate Between Objective Assessments and Performance Assessments

Plan your courses so you alternate between objective assessments and performance assessments. Objective assessments can require a lot of studying and mental energy, especially if the course involves complex materials. If you stack multiple exam-heavy classes back to back, it is very easy to burn out. Alternating between exam courses and the paper-based or project courses is a really good way to keep things balanced. It's not always going to line up perfectly, but after spending a lot of time studying for an exam, it actually feels really refreshing to switch to writing a paper or completing a project for the next course.

If you take the pre-assessment and pass it with a strong score, schedule the real objective assessment as soon as possible, ideally sometime in that same week. Once the exam is scheduled, use the time leading up to it to review your weakest areas, because the pre-assessment provides statistics that show which topics you struggle with. One method that works really well is going through every single pre-assessment question and making sure you can logically walk through every single part of that question and explain why the correct answer is correct.
